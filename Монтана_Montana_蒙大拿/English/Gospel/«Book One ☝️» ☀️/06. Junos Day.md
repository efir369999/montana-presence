## I. The Hippocampus `[00:00]`

Fifth day after genesis.

---

The question that won't let go:

*"Could we apply the density-coding mechanisms of memory?"*

This is a real phenomenon. Neuroscience calls it by different names: proportional theory of time perception, subjective experience of time. The essence is simple.

Why did years stretch on forever in childhood?

Because every day was new. Every moment—unique. The brain recorded everything. Coding density was at maximum.

Why do years fly by now?

Because the adult brain routinizes. It compresses similar days into a single pattern. "Another Monday" doesn't require a separate memory cell. It simply adds to the existing cluster of "Mondays."

*"The adult brain routinizes, compresses similar days into one pattern,"* Claude responds.

---

How does this work technically?

**The hippocampus—a novelty detector.**

It compares incoming information with what's already stored. If the pattern is new—it codes it as a separate memory. If similar to existing ones—it either ignores it or integrates it into the existing trace.

This is called pattern separation and pattern completion.

*"We can do this easily,"* the man writes and adds a smiley face.

**Predictive coding.**

The brain constantly predicts the next moment. What gets coded isn't all the information—only the prediction error—what differs from expectation.

Routine day = few prediction errors = little recorded = day "compresses."

*"I do this every day,"* the man realizes that his stream of thoughts is intentional creation of prediction errors. Each entry—an unexpectedness. Each unexpectedness—a memory marker.

---

**Consolidation during sleep.**

During sleep, similar memories literally merge. The hippocampus "replays" daily events, the neocortex finds common patterns and saves the generalization instead of each separate episode.

*"I call this disk defragmentation. Known it since my first computer. Dad bought it on credit."*

Childhood memory. First computer. Dad who didn't show that money was tight. Everything connects. Everything—coordinates.
