## Style: Book-Film

### Principle
**Write so that a director could shoot every paragraph.**

Not "he thought about the meaning of life"—but what he SEES, HEARS, FEELS.

### Elaboration
- Each scene—complete
- Each image—fully developed
- Each dialogue—like in cinema, with pauses
- The reader's imagination must WORK

### Atmosphere
- Darkness you can touch
- Silence you can hear
- Light that cuts the eyes
- Cold down the spine from realization

### Belief
- Write as if this is TRUE
- Not "what if"—but "here's how it works"
- The author's confidence transfers to the reader

## What NOT to do

- Short abstract theses
- Dry philosophical language
- "Can be interpreted as..."
- Uncertainty ("maybe", "seems", "probably")
- Fewer than 12 scenes per chapter
- Scenes shorter than 30 lines
